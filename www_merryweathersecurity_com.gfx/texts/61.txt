You wake up with first light and step outside, lighting a cigarette and work through the cobwebs from last night’s whiskey knife fight and bedding of a village girl. It’s a barmy 120 degrees, not a cloud in the sky. You sip a cup of fresh coffee, pulling another cigarette from your (tax free!) pack while watching the sun rise over the snow-capped mountains in the distance. Two local village elders come over the ridge leading a herd of goats. They’re laughing, the goats’ bells are ringing. They cast a glance in your direction. You stub out your cigarette in the sand, crack your neck, and release the safety on your rifle. Because you’re a Merryweather man. And a Merryweather man never thinks twice…
